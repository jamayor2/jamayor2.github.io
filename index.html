Research

Research

My research is focused on quantifying the soil moisture and evapotranspiration in dryland ecosystems (Southwestern US) through distributed hydrological simulations, remote sensing, and field measurements within the Jornada Experimental Range of the U.S. Department of
Agriculture in southern New Mexico, U.S.
The model inputs can be summarized into terrain, atmospheric forcing, soil, and vegetation parameters. A small size watershed (âˆ¼ 69 km2) has been delineated and selected as the study zone encompassing two Eddy Covariance (EC) stations and multi-layer and spatially distributed soil moisture sensors. The watershed divide was extracted using a 30 m resolution Digital Elevation Model (DEM) to derive the Triangular Irregular Network (TIN) that the model uses to run.
